Welcome to our Community

The Python Software Community Ghana (Python Ghana) is a body that embraces all friends of the Python programming language in Ghana. We are a community of master programmers, intermediate and even absolute beginners, but one thing we all have in common is the love of Python. It encompasses all friends of the Python programming language in Ghana.

With the aim to make a difference in our society we kicked off different initiatives to address and have impact in different areas in the society such as PyLadies Ghana to empower more ladies and introduce them to Tech using the Python Programming language, PyData Ghana for educating individuals in Data science and Artificial intelligence, PyScholars for the Senior high level and PyClubs where students meet and learn at the Tertiary level.

About Us and the Team


PyLadies Ghana is a Mentor-ship group in Ghana focused on helping more women become active participants and leaders in the Python open-source Community. It's an initiative to cultivate a more female-friendly environment in Python Software Community Ghana. We are open to everyone, not just ladies.

PyData Ghana brings together data loving #Pythonistas and Data Scientists, where we share ideas and learn from each other. PyData Ghana aims to promotes discussion of best practices, new approaches, and emerging technologies for data management, processing, analytics, and visualization. We approach data science using many languages, including (but not limited to) Python, Julia, and R. We aim to be an accessible, community-driven conference, with novice to advanced level presentations. PyData tutorials and talks bring attendees the latest project features along with cutting-edge use cases.

Python is a high-level programming language , released for the first time in 1991 by its creator Guido van Rossum , a Dutch programmer currently operating in Dropbox. It derives its name from the comedy Monty Python's Flying Circus of the famous Monty Python , aired on the BBC over the years 70. At present, the development of Python (and especially thanks to the enormous and dynamic international community of developers) is managed by the organization no-profit Python Software Foundation.
